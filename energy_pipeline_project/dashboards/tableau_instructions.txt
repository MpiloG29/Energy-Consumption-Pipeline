# Tableau Dashboard Instructions
# File: consumption_trends.twb (to be created in Tableau Desktop)

## DATA SOURCE:
1. Connect to: dashboard_data/hourly_energy_data.csv
2. Or use: dashboard_data/daily_energy_data.csv

## RECOMMENDED VISUALIZATIONS:

### Sheet 1: Time Series Analysis
- Chart Type: Line Chart
- X-Axis: date (continuous)
- Y-Axis: total_kwh (sum)
- Color: day_name (discrete)
- Title: "Daily Energy Consumption Trends"

### Sheet 2: Hourly Pattern Analysis  
- Chart Type: Bar Chart
- X-Axis: hour (discrete)
- Y-Axis: avg_kwh (average)
- Color: day_name
- Title: "Average Consumption by Hour of Day"

### Sheet 3: Day of Week Analysis
- Chart Type: Heat Map
- Rows: day_name
- Columns: hour
- Color: avg_kwh (average)
- Title: "Consumption Heatmap: Day vs Hour"

### Sheet 4: Statistical Summary
- Show Cards for:
  * Total Consumption (sum of total_kwh)
  * Average Hourly Consumption (avg of avg_kwh)
  * Peak Consumption (max of max_kwh)
  * Minimum Consumption (min of min_kwh)

### Dashboard: Energy Consumption Overview
Layout: 2x2 grid
1. Top Left: Time Series Analysis
2. Top Right: Hourly Pattern Analysis  
3. Bottom Left: Day of Week Heatmap
4. Bottom Right: Statistical Summary

## FILTERS TO ADD:
1. Date Range Slider
2. Day of Week Selector
3. Hour Range Slider

## CALCULATED FIELDS:
1. Consumption Category:
   IF [avg_kwh] < 2 THEN 'Low'
   ELSEIF [avg_kwh] < 2.5 THEN 'Medium'
   ELSE 'High'
   END

2. Peak Hours:
   IF [hour] >= 17 AND [hour] <= 21 THEN 'Evening Peak'
   ELSEIF [hour] >= 7 AND [hour] <= 10 THEN 'Morning Peak'
   ELSE 'Off-Peak'
   END
